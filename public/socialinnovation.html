WE ARE CURRENTLY SEARCHING FOR MOTIVATED AND DRIVEN TEAMS, AND INDIVIDUALS, WHO WOULD BE INTERESTED IN MAKING REAL CHANGE IN THE OSHAWA COMMUNITY.

The Covid-19 crisis has impacted businesses across a variety of sectors in the City of Oshawa. In April 2020, Mayor Dan Carter launched the Mayor’s Economic Recovery Task Force. The Mayor’s Task Force is working to identify actions to support the needs of Oshawa businesses impacted by COVID-19. The Task Force will focus on economic support and recovery for local businesses, including relief measures, resources and supports.

The City of Oshawa has challenged Ontario Tech students to come up with concepts or ventures to solve these problems focused on any one of challenge questions below - all related to the work of the Task Force.

Solutions could consist of applied research, non-profit ventures, social entrepreneurship, a product, a service, or other. Students will then compete, as teams, for the chance to implement their solution as a pilot project.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS


WHAT IF I DON'T HAVE A TEAM?

Individuals may apply and enter the competition alone. However, if you wish to find team members you can fill out an additional application form to join an existing team or find other individuals to create your own team.


CAN ANYONE ENTER?

Individuals may apply and enter the competition alone. However, if you wish to find team members you can fill out an additional application form to join an existing team or find other individuals to create your own team.


HOW DOES THE COMPETITION ASPECT WORK?

The teams will enter a series of two 3-day “sprints” in October where they will have the chance to further develop their solutions. These sprints will give teams a chance to get feedback and advice from the Brilliant Catalyst mentors, as well as get a crash-course in social entrepreneurship.

After the sprints, the teams will create ‘tangible’ evidence of their solution. This tangible evidence can be in the form of a fully developed business model and customer validation, a prototype or MVP, or even a formal research proposal.

In January, teams will compete in the Social Innovation Design Sprint Pitch Challenge, where they will be judged by representatives from the City of Oshawa Economic Task Force, Teaching City, and more.


WHAT DO YOU WIN?

The finalists from this competition will receive seed-funding to implement their project in the community as a pilot project. As a bonus, the teams who have most effectively demonstrated the effectiveness of their solutions by April 2021 will have the chance to receive additional funding and support to continue their venture for the future.

MAX AND MIN NUMBER OF PEOPLE ON A TEAM?

There is no maximum or minimum number of people allowed on a team.
